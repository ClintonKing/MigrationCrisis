on a watercolor background?
The sun sets behind the Sicilian mountains, which rise across the Strait of Messina. The water is calm, reflecting the light of the sun and only interrupted by small sailing boats and a colossal metallic ship anchored between the sunset and the port. Hundreds of refugees are waiting onboard, their silhouettes clearly marked by the sun behind them. We are in Reggio di Calabria, a small city in mainland southern Italy, at the other end of the strait. For the past couple of years this ancient city–once home to the Greek, Roman and Byzantine civilizations among others–has seen a different kind of cultural influx. Thousands of refugees looking for better lives have arrived after crossing the Mediterranean in perilous conditions in an enormous exodus that has become part of the biggest wave of global migration since the Second World War.
